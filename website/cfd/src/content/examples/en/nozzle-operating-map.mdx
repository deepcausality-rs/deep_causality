The first example with an actual solver behind the sweep. Each row runs a 1-D
compressible duct march over 128 cells; the sweep collects them into one table.

Geometry is a converging–diverging nozzle: inlet 2 m², throat 1 m², exit 2 m² over
1 m of length, fed from a 300 kPa / 500 K reservoir.

## The output

```text
  p_back/p0   M_exit   shock x [m]      Cf
      0.90    0.325       0.6562    0.242
      0.80    0.368       0.7656    0.277
      0.70    0.420       0.8516    0.316
      0.60    0.493       0.9297    0.365
      0.30    2.123         none    0.857
      0.10    2.123         none    1.254
```

Read the shock column downward. As back-pressure falls, the normal shock walks
downstream — 0.656 m, 0.766 m, 0.852 m, 0.930 m — and then leaves the duct entirely.
The last two rows are shock-free supersonic exits.

The exit Mach freezing at 2.123 across the last two rows is the physically correct
answer, and it is worth pausing on: once the nozzle is fully supersonic, the exit
Mach is fixed by the *area ratio* alone. Lowering back-pressure further changes the
plume outside the nozzle, not the flow inside it. The thrust coefficient keeps
climbing (0.857 → 1.254) because it depends on the pressure difference, which does
keep changing.

## Checked against the analytic values

```text
analytic references: first critical p/p0 = 0.9372, exit-plane shock at p/p0 = 0.5134
```

Those two numbers bracket the interesting regime and come from closed-form
quasi-1-D relations, not from the march. The gates use them: the choking gate
requires the throat to be choked below the first critical ratio, and the
shock-position gate requires the computed shock stations to fall between the throat
and the exit plane, walking monotonically downstream.

Measured exit Mach is 2.123 against a design 2.197 — about 3.4% low, which is the
signature of a first-order scheme smearing the expansion.

## Concurrency

Six marches run concurrently under the `parallel` feature, through
`deep_causality_par`. The rows are independent by construction, so the result is
order-preserving and identical to the serial run.

## Where this stops being enough

The duct march is first-order and quasi-1-D. Shocks smear over a few cells, which is
why the shock station is a cell-resolution estimate rather than a sharp location. The
gas is perfect at γ = 1.4 with no wall friction, no heat transfer and no real-gas
effects — all three matter in a real nozzle.

The sweep also stays inside the choked regimes. The unchoked window above the first
critical ratio is not covered, so this is an operating map for the design envelope,
not for startup transients.
