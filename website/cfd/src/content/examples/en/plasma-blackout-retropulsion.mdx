The [corridor](/examples/plasma-blackout-corridor/) stops at 47 km. The
[weather example](/examples/plasma-blackout-weather/) produces a dispersion
table. This example reads that table in flight, commits an ignition, and carries
the same vehicle to the ground under a retro burn.

Vehicle: 3400 kg wet, 2200 kg propellant, 70 kN central nozzle at Isp 282 s.

## Why supersonic retropropulsion is the hard case

Firing an engine forward into a supersonic freestream does not add thrust to a
fixed drag. The plume displaces the bow shock and destroys the aerodynamic drag
the vehicle was using. Jarvinen and Adams measured the collapse in 1970, and past
a thrust coefficient near 3 the interaction goes unsteady.

The consequence is that a vehicle which lights its engine too hard arrives
faster than one that coasts. That is the trade this example flies.

## Five acts

Every leg boundary is an event the run locates, either a flow-resolved crossing
or a guidance commit.

**Act 0, plan.** The measured day is 32 K below standard. Its departure
interpolates the weather table, which sizes the ignition margin at 73.41 m from
62.87 ± 3.51 m of drift at k = 3, supplies the density scale, and scales the
accelerometer bias.

**Act 1, corridor.** The inherited descent, flown with the burn stack composed
and the throttle commanded to zero. The thrust and plume stages are inert at zero
throttle, so composing them early costs nothing and keeps the safety envelope
live on every step.

Gate 1 checks the inheritance against the table for that day: blackout onset at
10.50 s against 10.54 s, dwell 59.50 s against 59.41 s, tolerance 0.50 s.

**Acts 2 and 3, coast, commit, burn.** One march call rather than two. A
coupling stack is fixed per call and the marched fluid tensor is not carried
across a leg boundary, so splitting at ignition would re-seed the flow and the
fork below would fork a state with the plume discarded.

The ignition corridor commits at 32.68 km, Mach 1.99, q 2224.9 Pa, on aided
navigation at sigma 0.382 m against the day's 73.41 m margin.

**Act 3b, burn.** Flown under the SRP envelope until Mach 0.6 at 18.50 km.

**Act 4, terminal.** Cutoff, a subsonic re-seed under its own gamma, then the
landing.

## The fork

The marched plume-coupled state is forked once per candidate throttle: a coast
branch, two straddling the drag sign-flip band, a nominal branch, and an
engine-degraded contingency.

`cmd` is what each world published; `flown` is what the envelope admitted. The
hard branch is the one case where the two differ.

## The drag sign flip

Ordered by realized throttle, net axial deceleration is non-monotone. Coasting
gives 10.595 m/s². Lighting the engine to 0.20 throttle gives 7.472 m/s², which
is *less*. Preserved drag collapses 0.2510 to −0.0611 across the burning
branches, reaching the correlation's negative wake-type branch at the harder
throttles.

In the low thrust-coefficient band the plume destroys preserved drag about as
fast as thrust replaces it. Gate 4b finds this from trajectory outcomes rather
than asserting it from the correlation.

## What the state fork earns

Gate 4c is the one that justifies forking state rather than parameters. Each
branch's realized velocity increment is compared against a frozen-drag
prediction: the same thrust schedule with the drag closure held at the fork's
value. The `dv frozen` column above is that prediction.

The branches depart it by up to 139.4 m/s against a 100 m/s threshold. Thrust-only
kinematics does not predict the outcome.

Gate 4a records a flow spread of 0.0202 across the roster against a 0.0150
threshold. The corridor's bank branches agreed to three digits; these do not.

Fork economics, gates 4d and 4g: all five branches entered by reference with no
tensor copied at fork time, worst post-fork bond growth 0 against a cap of 8, and
the fan-out marched at 1.11× the trunk per-step cost against a cap of 3.00.

## The belief counterfactual

The landing is flown twice from the same baseline on the same measured
atmosphere, differing only in the margin the guidance was sized with.

| world | margin | lights landing burn | contact | propellant |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| informed | 73.41 m | 153.19 m | 1.78 m/s | +13.45 kg |
| uninformed | 52.98 m | 129.75 m | 1.85 m/s | — |

The margin reaches the flight through `ignition_altitude_kernel`, which adds it
to the stopping distance, so a guidance that believes the day is more dispersed
lights its landing burn higher.

The flown separation is 23.45 m against an arithmetic margin difference of
20.43 m. The two differ because the kernel solves a stopping distance rather
than applying an offset, so the extra margin also changes the mass and speed the
burn starts from.

The example is explicit about where the margin does not bind: at the ignition
commit the navigated sigma is 0.38 m against margins of 73.41 m and 52.98 m, two
orders of magnitude inside either. Both beliefs commit on the same step. A gate
reading the commit would have reported the two worlds as identical.

## Three corrections recorded in constants.rs

Each is documented beside the band it superseded.

**The SRP envelope does not apply subsonically.** The C_T ≤ 3 cap and the
throttle floor are bow-shock-interaction statements from a dataset spanning
Mach 0.4 to 2.0. Carried to the deck they make the admissible throttle collapse
with dynamic pressure and forbid the engine around 3.7 km. Landing at 160 m/s
was a constraint applied outside its validity envelope. The terminal leg flies
its own subsonic envelope.

**Continuous throttle wastes propellant.** The closed form `a_cmd = v²/2h + g`
degenerates to `a_cmd ≈ g` at large `h`, which is thrust balancing weight. A
vehicle that commits high nulls its descent rate at altitude and then hovers.
This one ran dry at 10.5 km. Meditch (1964) showed the fuel-optimal control for
the soft-landing problem is bang-bang, so any sustained intermediate throttle is
wasteful by construction. The guidance now holds at zero until the altitude
reaches `ignition_altitude_kernel`'s answer.

**A lander arrives moving.** The stopping law targets the gear contact plane at
a commanded contact speed rather than the geocenter at rest.

## Applicability limits

**Drag authority is the correlation, not the field.** The M1 de-risk measurement
returned AMBER on imprint fidelity: a compressible forcing region does not
reproduce the Jarvinen–Adams collapse at this resolution. The in-flight drag
decrement is the cited A0 correlation evaluated at each branch's thrust
coefficient. The `PlumeImprint` seam gives the marched layer state realism and
nothing further. See [capability boundaries](/boundaries/#srp).

**The two SRP models barely overlap.** Jarvinen–Adams covers Mach 0.4 to 2.0;
Cordell–Braun validated the plume boundary over Mach 2 to 4. They meet at a
point. The burn flies the correlation's band, so the plume geometry driving the
marched-layer imprint sits outside its own model's envelope for most of it.
Gate 4a's flow spread therefore measures throttle → trajectory → post-shock
density rather than a plume footprint. The example states that it is not the
imprint witness the design note anticipated.

**Gate 4d cannot be falsified by input.** The two sharing flags compare a clone
against the `Arc` it was cloned from. They guard against a future edit that
materializes the state instead of sharing it. The quantity that varies with the
run is the post-fork bond growth, which the gate bands, measured 0. An earlier
README overclaimed a reference count as positive evidence; that claim was
withdrawn.

**Day-of-entry targeting is not implemented, and the measurement is the reason.**
A colder day ionizes earlier and dwells longer, and the two effects cancel:
across the tabulated range onset swings 4.2 s and dwell swings 3.1 s, while
blackout exit lands within 1.1 s on every day. The gap that a targeting shift
would protect runs from exit at ~70 s to the commit at 224 s, perturbed by 1.1 s.
The table's information is in the drift column, which swings 32.7 m, and the
ignition margin already consumes it. A heavier ballistic bundle would reach the
ignition band lower and later and squeeze that gap.

**On-axis magnitudes only.** No angle of attack in the roster, so the lateral
plume–shock interaction is out of frame.

**Tier-A closed-form guidance.** Apollo polynomial guidance (Klumpp 1974) and
convex powered-descent guidance (Açıkmeşe and Ploen 2007) are the named upgrade
path.

The corridor limitations carry over unchanged: single-point sheath chemistry,
compressed time, a 2-D marched layer, and a deterministic 3-DOF flight world
with no winds or aero dispersions.
