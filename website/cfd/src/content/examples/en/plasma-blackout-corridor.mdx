In about forty seconds this example marches a compressible flow, reacts its
plasma chemistry, gates a Kalman filter on the result, forks the marched state
into seventeen counterfactual worlds, and reports thirteen gate verdicts.

## The problem

A vehicle reenters at Mach 25. The shock layer ionizes, the electron density climbs
past the GPS L1 cutoff, and the vehicle loses GNSS — radio blackout. It flies blind
on inertial navigation until recombination drains the sheath. Then it reacquires,
with whatever navigation error accumulated in the dark.

The guidance problem is to select a bank angle at the decision point. The
navigation state available at that point is degraded by an amount that depends on
the blackout duration, which depends on the flow.

## Blackout window located by the chemistry

The window is not a config parameter. The run marches until the evolved sheath's
electron density crosses the L1 cutoff:

```rust
let onset = CfdFlow::march(&nominal)
    .couple(world::corridor_coupling(1.0, 0))
    .trigger(trigger)
    .from_field(world::initial_field())
    .until(|field, _| field.regime().map(|r| r.gnss_denied).unwrap_or(false))?;
```

```text
[PASS] (1) flow-resolved blackout window: onset at step 119 (74.7 km),
       denied through the peak, exit at 47.0 km after a 58.4 s dwell
```

The chemistry is anchored against flight data:

```text
[PASS] (2) peak n_e vs the RAM-C II anchor: n_e = 3.349e19 m^-3 at the 60.9 km
       passage, in [2.0e18, 5.0e19] around the flight anchor 1e19 m^-3
```

Exit at 47.0 km sits above the RAM-C II flight window of 25 to 30 km. The example
attributes the offset to the probe's light ballistic bundle, which decelerates at
higher altitude, rather than to the chemistry.

## Counterfactual branches from the paused state

At onset the march pauses. That pause is forked once per candidate bank angle, and
each branch continues from the shared state in its own alternated world.

The fork is O(1) copy-on-write, covering tensor fields, navigation engine and
provenance log. Each branch takes its clone at its first write. Every branch
carries an `!!ContextAlternation!!` marker naming its baseline.

Six coarse branches, then eleven refined:

```text
 0.0deg  20.000 m
 5.0deg  12.812 m
10.0deg   5.807 m
15.0deg   3.120 m   <- committed from the coarse round
20.0deg   9.612 m
40.0deg  22.018 m

fine winner: 13.5deg  2.393 m
```

```text
[PASS] (4e) guidance precision from the sweep: committed 13.5 deg lands 2.39 m
       off the aim vs the ballistic 20.00 m (8.4x better; gate requires 3x)
```

The response is not monotone: 40° lands 22.018 m from the aim point, worse than
the 20.000 m zero-bank case.

## Navigation through the denial window

```text
[PASS] (3) real INS drift -> reacquisition: err 0.3467 m -> 2.5448 m
       dead-reckoning to the peak passage, 0.2775 m after reacquisition
```

A 17-state error-state Kalman filter dead-reckons through the denial window.
Error grows by a factor of about seven, then falls below its starting value once
fixes return. The commit decision uses the degraded value, which is why the
blackout duration produced by the flow solve enters the guidance result.

## Precision

| Type | Result |
|---|---|
| `f64` | all gates pass in ~35 s |
| `Float106` | identical events, 15–16 significant digits, ~11× wall-clock |
| `f32` | crashes at step 1 |

The `f32` failure is a representability limit rather than an accuracy limit: `h²`
in the then-flown Saha kernel evaluates to 4.4e-67, below the `f32` exponent
range.

## Applicability limits

The sheath closure is single-point with `T_e = T_ve` lumped. There is no
spatially resolved reacting layer.

Time is compressed: each coupled step represents 0.1 s of flight.

The marched layer is 2-D. A [timing study](/boundaries/#timing-3d) measured the
3-D fitted marcher at 3.6× over the wall-clock budget.

The run carries no winds, no aero-coefficient dispersions and no density
perturbations. The
[weather dispersion table](/examples/plasma-blackout-weather/) supplies the
temperature and density axes.
