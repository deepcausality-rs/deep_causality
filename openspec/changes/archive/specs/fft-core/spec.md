## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Dedicated zero-dependency FFT crate
The workspace SHALL contain a crate `deep_causality_fft` whose only workspace
dependency is `deep_causality_num` and which declares no external
dependencies. The crate SHALL opt into the workspace lint policy
(`[lints] workspace = true`, hence `unsafe_code = "forbid"`) and SHALL NOT
join the documented unsafe-exemption list. All transforms SHALL be generic
over `R: RealField` and operate on `Complex<R>` from `deep_causality_num`;
the crate SHALL NOT define numeric types of its own.

#### Scenario: Crate compiles under the workspace lint policy
- **WHEN** the workspace builds with `deep_causality_fft` as a member
- **THEN** the crate compiles with `unsafe_code = "forbid"` in effect and `cargo tree` shows `deep_causality_num` as its only dependency

#### Scenario: Transforms are precision-generic
- **WHEN** the same transform is instantiated at f32 and f64
- **THEN** both compile and produce results matching the naïve DFT reference within the per-precision accuracy bound

### Requirement: Plan-based forward complex FFT
The crate SHALL provide a plan type (`FftPlan<R>`) constructed once per
transform length that precomputes twiddle factors, permutation, and stage
schedule, and an execute operation over `Complex<R>` buffers. Plans SHALL be
immutable after construction. Execution SHALL perform no heap allocation;
any scratch space SHALL be provided or reusable by the caller. The planner
SHALL select: hardcoded straight-line butterflies for small power-of-two
lengths (at minimum 2, 4, 8, 16, 32), an iterative mixed radix-2/radix-4
pipeline for larger powers of two, and the Bluestein fallback for all other
lengths, so that every length N is transformed in O(N log N).

#### Scenario: Forward transform matches the naïve DFT
- **WHEN** a forward FFT of any planned length (small butterfly, power-of-two pipeline, and a non-power-of-two Bluestein length) is compared against the naïve O(n²) DFT on the same input
- **THEN** all outputs agree within the documented accuracy bound for the precision

#### Scenario: Repeated execution does not allocate
- **WHEN** a plan is executed repeatedly with caller-supplied buffers
- **THEN** no per-execution heap allocation occurs

#### Scenario: Non-power-of-two lengths stay O(N log N)
- **WHEN** a prime-length transform is planned
- **THEN** the planner selects the Bluestein fallback rather than a quadratic algorithm and the result matches the naïve DFT within the accuracy bound

### Requirement: Inverse FFT by conjugation reuse with a fixed normalization contract
The inverse transform SHALL be implemented by conjugation reuse of the
forward kernel (`ifft(x) = conj(fft(conj(x))) / N`), not as a separate
inverse butterfly kernel. The normalization convention SHALL be: forward
transform unnormalized, inverse transform scaled by `1/N`. The convention
SHALL be documented on every public transform entry point.

#### Scenario: Round-trip identity
- **WHEN** `ifft(fft(x))` is computed for arbitrary input at every planned length class
- **THEN** the result equals `x` to within rounding for the precision

#### Scenario: Inverse matches the conjugate-reuse definition
- **WHEN** the inverse transform is compared against `conj(fft(conj(x))) / N` computed by hand from the forward plan
- **THEN** the two are bit-identical

### Requirement: Naïve DFT validation reference
The crate SHALL ship a naïve O(n²) DFT used as the correctness reference in
tests. It SHALL never be selected by the planner.

#### Scenario: Reference is test-only on the dispatch path
- **WHEN** any length is planned
- **THEN** the selected algorithm is never the naïve DFT

### Requirement: Numerically careful twiddle generation across precisions
Twiddle factors SHALL be generated by a scheme whose error does not grow
linearly with naive angle accumulation (exact anchors with bounded-length
recurrences, in the manner of PocketFFT), using only `RealField` +
`FromPrimitive` operations, so that accuracy holds for every supported
precision including extended-precision backends without a native `sincos`.

#### Scenario: Accuracy at large transform lengths
- **WHEN** a forward transform at the largest benchmarked power-of-two length is compared against the naïve DFT computed at higher precision
- **THEN** the relative error stays within the documented per-precision bound

### Requirement: Stand-alone FFT benchmark
The crate SHALL provide a criterion benchmark covering 1-D and 3-D transforms
at the solver-relevant sizes (16³, 32³, 64³ grids and their per-axis lengths)
plus at least one Bluestein-path size, at f32 and f64.

#### Scenario: Benchmark runs
- **WHEN** the crate's benchmark target is executed
- **THEN** it reports timings for the power-of-two and Bluestein paths without error
